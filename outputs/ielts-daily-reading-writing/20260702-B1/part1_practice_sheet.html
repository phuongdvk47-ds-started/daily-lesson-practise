I. Reading Passage
Japanese Town Blocks Mount Fuji View to Control Overtourism

The Japanese town of Fujikawaguchiko, which sits southwest of Tokyo, has recently taken extreme measures to control crowds of visitors. Local officials have started building a large black screen to block views of Mount Fuji, the country's tallest mountain. While the town is famous for having beautiful photo spots, residents have complained about the unprecedented [R] number of tourists who behave badly.

Many business owners and locals feel that the behavior of some tourists has become a public nuisance [R]. According to residents, visitors often cross the road in busy traffic, leave waste on streets, ignore stop lights, and trespass onto private property. Michie Motomochi, who owns a traditional sweets restaurant, commented that while tourism drives eighty percent of her business, she is worried about the lack of respect shown by some travelers.

The neighborhood suddenly became a viral sensation about two years ago. An online travel blogger decided to recommend [R] a specific photo spot on social media. The photo showed Mount Fuji in the background, appearing to sit directly on top of a local Lawson convenience store. Since then, foreign tourists have crowded the area daily, starting as early as four in the morning and talking loudly.

Before building the barrier, the town tried other methods to limit bad behavior. They put up signs in several languages, provided information about crosswalks, and even hired security guards. However, these attempts failed to stop the crowds. The black screen, which stands 2.5 meters high and 20 meters long, will almost completely block the famous mountain view.

While some foreign tourists believe the screen is an overreaction by city officials, many locals feel it is necessary. Yoshihiko Ogawa, who runs a rice shop, explained that the crowds had worsened, blocking his car and causing noise. He admitted that he was unsure about the best solution, adding, "I suppose we all just need to get used to it."
