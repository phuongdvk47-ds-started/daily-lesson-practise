I. Reading Passage
Dressing for the Weather

Do you look out the window before you get dressed in the morning? Most people do. This is because the weather helps us decide what to wear. Weather refers to what is happening outside right now. For example, if it is raining, you might need a rain jacket and an umbrella. If it is hot and sunny, you usually wear a lightweight T-shirt and shorts. Making the right choice helps you stay comfortable all day.

However, climate is different from weather. Climate means the usual weather in a place over a long time. People who live in hot climates usually have many thin clothes made of cotton. This material helps keep the body cool. On the other hand, people in cold climates must protect themselves from the freezing temperatures. They wear thick sweaters, gloves, and warm hats. Sometimes, they wear several layers [R] of clothing at the same time to trap the heat.

Clothing choices can also affect the environment. Washing and drying clothes use a lot of water and electricity. Some families now choose to hang their wet clothes outside on a sunny day instead of using a machine. This simple habit [R] can help save energy. Also, when we buy fewer clothes and wear them for a long time, we can reduce [R] the amount of waste we produce. In this way, our daily clothes are very important [R] for the planet.
