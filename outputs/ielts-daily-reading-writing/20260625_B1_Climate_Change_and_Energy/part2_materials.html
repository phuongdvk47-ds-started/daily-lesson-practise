VOCABULARY & GRAMMAR KNOWLEDGE - DAY 20260625: CLIMATE CHANGE & ENERGY

I. Vocabulary & Idioms Tables
Table 2.1: Academic Vocabulary (Từ vựng học thuật)
Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Loại từ Định nghĩa & Nghĩa tiếng Việt Ví dụ minh họa
agency /ˈeɪ.dʒən.si/ n An organization that provides a particular service on behalf of a government or group.
Cơ quan, tổ chức.		 The European climate agency released new global temperature data.
pre-industrial /ˌpriː.ɪnˈdʌs.tri.əl/ adj Relating to the time before factories and machinery were widely built (mid-1800s).
Trước thời kỳ công nghiệp.		 Temperatures last year were 1.48 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels.
exceed /ɪkˈsiːd/ v To be greater or more than a number, limit, or amount.
Vượt quá, vượt qua.		 Exceeding the climate limit could lead to severe disasters.
threshold /ˈθreʃ.həʊld/ n The level or point at which something starts to happen or take effect.
Ngưỡng, giới hạn.		 Scientists warn that crossing the 1.5-degree threshold will cause severe damage.
average /ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ/ n, adj A standard or typical level, calculated by adding quantities and dividing by their number.
Trung bình, mức trung bình.		 The global average temperature has risen significantly over the past century.
consecutive /kənˈsek.jə.tɪv/ adj Following one after another without an interruption or break.
Liên tiếp, liên tục.		 Earth broke global heat records for seven consecutive months.
exceptional /ɪkˈsep.ʃən.əl/ adj Much greater or better than usual; highly unusual or outstanding.
Đặc biệt, ngoại lệ, khác thường.		 The extreme temperatures were exceptional for over half the year.
pattern /ˈpæt.ən/ n A regular and repeated way in which something happens or is done.
Kiểu, mô hình, quy luật.		 El Niño is a natural weather pattern that warms parts of the Pacific Ocean.
release /rɪˈliːs/ v To allow a gas or substance to escape from a closed space into the air.
Thải ra, giải phóng.		 Fossil fuels release greenhouse gases when they are burned for energy.
limit /ˈlɪm.ɪt/ n, v The greatest amount or level of something that is allowed or possible.
Giới hạn, hạn chế.		 The Paris climate accord established a clear warming limit.
disaster /dɪˈzɑː.stər/ n An unexpected event, such as a flood or fire, that causes great harm, damage, or death.
Thảm họa, thiên tai.		 Global warming could lead to extreme climate disasters.
urge /ɜːdʒ/ v To strongly advise, request, or encourage someone to take action.
Thúc giục, kêu gọi.		 Environmental leaders urge countries to reduce fossil fuel use immediately.
record-breaking /ˈrek.ɔːdˌbreɪ.kɪŋ/ adj Surpassing any previous records in size, duration, or speed.
Phá kỷ lục, vượt kỷ lục.		 The record-breaking warmth lasted for seven consecutive months.
pre-industrial /ˌpriː.ɪnˈdʌs.tri.əl/ adj Existing or occurring before the development of modern industry.
Trước thời kỳ công nghiệp.		 We compare modern temperatures with the pre-industrial average.
long-term /ˌlɒŋˈtɜːm/ adj Continuing, lasting, or planned for a long period of time in the future.
Dài hạn, lâu dài.		 The Paris climate accord aims to limit long-term warming.
Table 2.2: Compound Words Table (Từ ghép)
Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Loại từ Định nghĩa & Nghĩa tiếng Việt Ví dụ minh họa
greenhouse /ˈɡriːn.haʊs/ n, adj A glass structure used for growing plants; relating to gases that trap heat in Earth's atmosphere.
Nhà kính, (khí) nhà kính.		 Carbon dioxide is the most common greenhouse gas produced by humans.
greenhouse gas /ˈɡriːn.haʊs ˌɡæs/ n A gas in the atmosphere that traps heat, contributing to global warming.
Khí nhà kính.		 Carbon dioxide is a major greenhouse gas produced by factories.
fossil fuel /ˈfɒs.əl ˌfjʊəl/ n A natural fuel such as coal, gas, or oil, formed in the geological past.
Nhiên liệu hóa thạch.		 Burning fossil fuel is the primary driver of climate change.
Table 2.3: Idioms & Phrases Table (Thành ngữ / Cụm từ cố định)
Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Loại từ Định nghĩa & Nghĩa tiếng Việt Ví dụ minh họa
shatter /ˈʃæt.ər/ v To break suddenly into very small pieces; to smash a record completely.
Đập tan, phá vỡ (kỷ lục).		 Earth shattered global heat records in 2023.
record /ˈrek.ɔːd/ n The best, fastest, or most extreme achievement in a field.
Kỷ lục.		 2023 was the warmest year on record.
weather pattern /ˈweð.ə ˌpæt.ən/ n Repeated weather conditions or cycles in a specific region.
Kiểu thời tiết, xu hướng thời tiết.		 El Niño is a weather pattern that causes higher temperatures globally.
tumble like dominoes /ˈtʌm.bəl laɪk ˈdɒm.ɪ.nəʊz/ idiom To fall, fail, or be broken one after another in rapid succession.
Đổ nhào như quân cờ đô-mi-nô.		 In 2023, global temperature records tumbled like dominoes.
flirt with /flɜːt wɪð/ phrase To come very close to reaching a certain level, threshold, or limit.
Tiệm cận, "mấp mé", cận kề.		 Global temperatures last year were flirting with the 1.5-degree limit.
at risk /æt rɪsk/ phrase In a dangerous situation where harm, loss, or death is possible.
Gặp rủi ro, gặp nguy hiểm.		 Human lives are at risk if we do not limit global warming quickly.
