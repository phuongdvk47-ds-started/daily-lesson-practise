II. Grammar Detail & Strategy (Lý thuyết & Mẹo)
Chủ điểm 1: Card 1: Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Use Past Simple for actions completed at a specific time in the past (yesterday, last year, in 2020). Use Present Perfect for actions connected to the present or occurring at an unstated time (since, yet, so far, up to now).

Tài liệu tự học trực tuyến uy tín:

Chủ điểm 2: Card 2: Gerunds and Infinitives after Verbs

Some verbs require a Gerund (-ing form) like avoid, enjoy, mind, suggest, recommend. Other verbs require a to-infinitive like decide, hope, manage, ask someone.

III. Common Mistakes / IELTS Traps for This Topic
Common mistake / Trap Wrong example Correct version Why it matters for IELTS
Using present perfect with specific past time words I have graduated in 2022. I graduated in 2022. 'in 2022' is a completed time period, so Past Simple must be used.
Using to-infinitive instead of gerund after avoid/suggest The manager suggested to hire him. The manager suggested hiring him. 'suggest' requires the gerund form for grammatical correctness.
Using present continuous for long-term continuous states starting in the past I am working here for three years. I have worked here for three years. 'for three years' signals that the state started in the past and continues, which requires Present Perfect.
