I. Reading Passage
Arts Program in Poor Performing Schools Helping Students

Many public schools in the United States struggle with low student attendance and poor test scores. To solve this challenge, a special program called "Turnaround Arts" started helping these schools. The program integrates art and music into the daily curriculum instead of teaching only reading and math.

Teachers use theater, painting, and musical instruments in their lessons to encourage student engagement. For example, in math class, students might use beats to learn fractions. As a result, students become more active and excited to participate in school activities. The program helps prepare [R] students for a better learning environment.

A study showed that schools using this program saw a major turnaround. Student performance in math and reading improved significantly. In addition, student behavior became better, and school attendance increased. Teachers recommend [R] this method because it boosts creativity and helps children express their emotional feelings. Today, many educators agree that children benefit [R] greatly from a successful arts education.

Source: Adapted from VOA Learning English - "Arts Program in Poor Performing Schools Helping Students" (May 2, 2017)
