III. Writing Guidance / Suggested Answers
Task 1: Rearrange the words to make correct sentences (Questions 1-5)
  • 1. The Iberian lynx is an endangered cat.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Hello! Let's look at how we build this sentence together. In English, we always start with the Subject (who or what does the action?). Here, our subject is "The Iberian lynx". Next, we need the Verb (what does it do or what is it?). The verb is "is". Finally, we add the complement description "an endangered cat" (the adjective "endangered" comes before the noun "cat"). Put them together, capitalize the first letter, and add a period at the end: The Iberian lynx is an endangered cat.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Check if you capitalized "The" and "Iberian" (since it is a proper adjective derived from Spain/Portugal's peninsula) and placed a period . at the end.
  • 2. We must protect their natural habitats.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Let's think about this step-by-step. Our subject is "We". Next, we use the modal verb "must" followed by a base verb "protect" (this shows a strong recommendation or necessity). What do we protect? "their natural habitats" (adjective "natural" comes before the noun "habitats"). Combining these gives us: We must protect their natural habitats.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Ensure you wrote "must protect" without adding "to" (avoid writing "must to protect").
  • 3. Wild rabbits are the main prey of lynx.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Here, the subject is plural: "Wild rabbits". Since the subject is plural, we use the plural verb "are". Next, we add the object/complement: "the main prey of lynx" (prey means an animal hunted for food). Put them together: Wild rabbits are the main prey of lynx.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Verify that you used "are" (for plural rabbits) and not "is".
  • 4. Many wild animals live in the forest.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Identify the parts of speech. "Many wild animals" is the plural subject. The action verb is "live" (no 's' at the end because the subject is plural). Where do they live? "in the forest". This gives us: Many wild animals live in the forest.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Make sure you write "live" and not "lives", since "animals" is plural.
  • 5. Dinosaurs died out millions of years ago.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Let's look at this historical fact. The subject is "Dinosaurs". The action verb is the phrasal verb "died out" (meaning became extinct). Finally, we specify the time phrase: "millions of years ago". This gives us: Dinosaurs died out millions of years ago.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Check that "died out" is in the past tense since dinosaurs are already extinct.
Task 2: Complete the sentences below using your own words (Questions 6-10)
  • 6. We should protect wildlife from extinction.
  • Teacher's Guidance: The prompt starts with "We should". After "should", you must use a base verb. The required words are "protect" (verb) and "wildlife" (noun). A great structure is: We should protect (base verb) + wildlife (noun) + from extinction (prepositional phrase showing the purpose).
  • Self-Correction Tip: Do not write "should to protect" or "should protecting".
  • 7. Many wild animals are extinct now because humans hunted them.
  • Teacher's Guidance: The sentence starts with "Many wild animals are". Here, "are" is a linking verb, so it must be followed by an adjective. The required word is "extinct" (adjective). To make the sentence complete and rich, add a reason using "because": Many wild animals are extinct now because humans hunted them.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Make sure you spell "extinct" correctly and check that the verb in the "because" clause is in the past tense ("hunted") since the hunting happened in the past.
  • 8. Animals need food to survive in the cold winter.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Starting with "Animals need food to", the word "to" here acts as an infinitive marker, so you must follow it with a base verb. The required word is "survive" (verb). You can add a details phrase like "in the wild" or "in the cold winter": Animals need food to survive in the cold winter.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Verify that "survive" is in its base form (no "-ing" or "-s" after "to").
  • 9. We must preserve natural forests for wild tigers.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Starting with "We must", you must follow it with a base verb. The required words are "preserve" (verb) and "forests" (noun). A beautiful sentence would be: We must preserve (base verb) + natural forests (noun phrase) + for wild tigers (beneficiary phrase).
  • Self-Correction Tip: Ensure you spelled "preserve" correctly and that you did not add "to" after "must".
  • 10. The population of lynx did not decline because they had enough prey.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Starting with "The population of lynx did not", the auxiliary "did not" must be followed by a base verb. The required words are "decline" (verb) and "prey" (noun). We can write: The population of lynx did not decline (base verb) + because they had enough prey (clause with noun).
  • Self-Correction Tip: Check that "decline" has no "-ed" or "-s" after "did not", and that "prey" is spelled correctly.
