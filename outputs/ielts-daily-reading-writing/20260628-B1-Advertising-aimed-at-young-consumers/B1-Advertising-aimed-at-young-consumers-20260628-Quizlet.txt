advertiser (/ˈædvətaɪzə/) [n]	A company or person that pays to promote a product or service - Nhà quảng cáo. Ví dụ: Advertisers pay social media platforms billions of dollars each year.
advertisement (/ədˈvɜːtɪsmənt/) [n]	A notice or display promoting a product, service, or event - Quảng cáo. Ví dụ: She clicked on an advertisement for a new brand of trainers.
algorithm (/ˈælɡərɪðəm/) [n]	A set of rules or calculations used by a computer to complete a task - Thuật toán. Ví dụ: The algorithm decides which posts you see first on your feed.
revenue (/ˈrevənjuː/) [n]	Money a company earns, e.g. from advertising - Doanh thu. Ví dụ: Social media companies earn most of their revenue from advertising.
adolescent (/ˌædəˈlesnt/) [n/adj]	A person aged roughly 10–19; relating to this age - Thiếu niên, tuổi thiếu niên. Ví dụ: Adolescents are particularly vulnerable to the influence of social media.
vulnerable (/ˈvʌlnərəbl/) [adj]	At risk of harm or easily affected by something negative - Dễ bị tổn thương. Ví dụ: Young children are more vulnerable to advertising than adults.
promote (/prəˈməʊt/) [v]	To support or publicise something; to increase awareness or sales - Thúc đẩy, quảng bá. Ví dụ: Algorithms promote content that keeps users on the platform for longer.
targeted advertising (/ˈtɑːɡɪtɪd ˈædvətaɪzɪŋ/) [n phrase]	Advertising designed specifically for a person based on their data - Quảng cáo nhắm mục tiêu. Ví dụ: Targeted advertising feels very personal because it uses your browsing history.
consumer behaviour (/kənˈsjuːmə bɪˈheɪvjə/) [n phrase]	The way in which people spend money and make purchasing decisions - Hành vi người tiêu dùng. Ví dụ: Marketers study consumer behaviour to create more effective campaigns.
onus (/ˈəʊnəs/) [n]	The responsibility or duty to do something - Trách nhiệm, nghĩa vụ. Ví dụ: The onus is on governments to regulate harmful platforms.
regulate (/ˈreɡjʊleɪt/) [v]	To control something using official rules or laws - Điều tiết, kiểm soát. Ví dụ: Experts say the government should regulate social media companies more strictly.
addictive (/əˈdɪktɪv/) [adj]	Causing a strong desire to continue doing something, making it hard to stop - Gây nghiện. Ví dụ: The design of many apps is deliberately addictive to keep users engaged.
screen time (/skriːn taɪm/) [n phrase]	The amount of time spent using a device with a screen - Thời gian dùng màn hình. Ví dụ: Doctors recommend limiting screen time for children to less than two hours per day.
engage (/ɪnˈɡeɪdʒ/) [v]	To attract and keep someone's attention or participation - Thu hút, gắn kết. Ví dụ: Platforms use videos and notifications to engage users for longer periods.
a losing battle (/ə ˈluːzɪŋ ˈbætl/) [idiom]	A struggle that you cannot win - Cuộc chiến vô vọng. Ví dụ: Trying to stop children from using social media feels like a losing battle for many parents.
fed (content) (/fed/) [v (past)]	Given a stream of content selected by a platform - Được cung cấp nội dung. Ví dụ: Every time she opened the app, she was fed videos that matched her interests.
persuasive (/pəˈsweɪsɪv/) [adj]	Able to convince someone to do or believe something - Có tính thuyết phục. Ví dụ: Targeted advertisements are often more persuasive than traditional TV commercials.
mental health (/ˈmentl helθ/) [n phrase]	A person's emotional and psychological wellbeing - Sức khỏe tâm thần. Ví dụ: Poor mental health among teenagers has been linked to excessive social media use.
play a role in (/pleɪ ə rəʊl ɪn/) [v phrase]	To be involved in or contribute to something - Đóng vai trò trong. Ví dụ: Social media algorithms play a major role in shaping what young people believe.
in the long run (/ɪn ðə lɒŋ rʌn/) [idiom]	Over a long period of time; eventually - Về lâu dài. Ví dụ: Reducing screen time may be difficult at first, but it is beneficial in the long run.
