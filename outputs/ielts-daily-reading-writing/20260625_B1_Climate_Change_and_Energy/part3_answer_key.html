III. Writing Guidance / Suggested Answers
Task 1: Rearrange the words to make correct sentences (Questions 1-5)
  • 1. Earth broke global heat records in 2023.
  • Teacher's Guidance: In English, we start with the Subject (who or what did it?). Here, it is "Earth" (capitalize it!). Next, we need the Verb (what did it do?). The past tense action is "broke". Next, we add the Object (what did it break?) which is "global heat records". Finally, we specify the year: "in 2023". Put them together: Earth broke global heat records in 2023.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Make sure you capitalized "Earth" and wrote "in 2023" at the end with a period.
  • 2. Greenhouse gases cause rising temperatures.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Our subject is plural: "Greenhouse gases". Next, we need the active verb: "cause". What do they cause? "rising temperatures" (noun phrase). Combining these gives: Greenhouse gases cause rising temperatures.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Verify that "cause" has no "s" at the end since "gases" is plural.
  • 3. We can use solar power to generate electricity.
  • Teacher's Guidance: The subject is "We". Next, we use the modal "can" + base verb "use". What do we use? "solar power". Why? "to generate electricity" (to + base verb + object). This gives: We can use solar power to generate electricity.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Check the spelling of "generate" and "electricity".
  • 4. Burning fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Here, our subject is a gerund phrase: "Burning fossil fuels". A gerund subject is always treated as singular, so the verb must have an 's': "releases". What does it release? "carbon dioxide". Put it together: Burning fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Double-check that you wrote "releases" (singular verb) and not "release", because "Burning" is the main singular subject.
  • 5. We must protect the climate of Earth.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Identify the subject: "We". Next, we use the modal "must" + base verb "protect". What do we protect? "the climate of Earth". This gives: We must protect the climate of Earth.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Verify that you capitalized "Earth" and spelled "climate" correctly.
Task 2: Complete the sentences using the words in parentheses (Questions 6-8)
  • 6. We can use wind energy to generate electricity.
  • Teacher's Guidance: The sentence starts with "We can use wind energy to". The word "to" indicates purpose, so it must be followed by a base verb. Use "generate electricity": We can use wind energy to generate electricity.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Do not add "-s" or "-ing" to "generate".
  • 7. If we burn fossil fuels, we will release greenhouse gases.
  • Teacher's Guidance: This is a First Conditional sentence (If + present simple, will + base verb). The first clause is "If we burn fossil fuels", so the second clause must start with a comma and use "we will release greenhouse gases". Put them together: If we burn fossil fuels, we will release greenhouse gases.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Check that you placed a comma , after the "If" clause, and used "will release" (not "will released").
  • 8. Solar panels are renewable energy sources.
  • Teacher's Guidance: The sentence starts with "Solar panels are". Since "are" is a linking verb and the subject is plural, follow it with a plural noun phrase. Use "renewable energy sources": Solar panels are renewable energy sources.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Make sure you used the plural form "sources".
Task 3: Translate the sentences into English (Questions 9-10)
  • 9. Climate change is making droughts more severe.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Let's translate this step-by-step. "Biến đổi khí hậu" is "Climate change" (subject). "đang làm cho" is "is making" (present continuous). "hạn hán" is "droughts" (object). "trở nên nghiêm trọng hơn" is "more severe" (adjective). The structure is Subject + make + Object + Adjective. Putting it together: Climate change is making droughts more severe.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Double-check the spelling of "droughts" and "severe".
  • 10. We must find a solution to global warming.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Let's translate: "Chúng ta" is "We". "phải" is "must" (modal). "tìm kiếm" is "find". "giải pháp" is "a solution". "cho sự nóng lên toàn cầu" is "to global warming" (in English, the preposition after "solution" is "to", not "for"). Put it together: We must find a solution to global warming.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Ensure you used the preposition "to" after "solution" (solution to...).
