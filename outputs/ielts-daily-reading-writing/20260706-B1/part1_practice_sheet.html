I. Reading Passage
It's Alarming: What Wakes You Up Each Morning?

Every morning, millions of people wake up to the sound of an alarm. For many, this is a difficult part of their daily routine [R]. Traditional alarm clocks use a loud, unpleasant beep or other harsh sounds to wake people up. However, researchers suggest that these sounds might not be the most effective [R] way to start your day. Instead, they can make you feel tired and unfocused.

When we wake up, we often feel heavy and sleepy. Scientists call this inertia or grogginess. They found that people who wake up to harsh beeps feel groggy for a longer time. For most people, this feeling lasts for about 30 minutes, but for some, it can persist for hours. This makes it hard to focus on work or school. A study in Australia of 50 people showed that waking up to a melodic sound, like a popular song or a pleasant tune, can help. A melody increases our attention and helps us transition from sleep to waking up. As a result, people feel refreshed and more alert.

During sleep, the brain repairs its synapses, which are connections between brain cells. There are also other ways to get a better sleep. Doctors recommend good sleep hygiene. This includes keeping the bedroom dark and quiet. You should also limit your screen time before bed. If you experience [R] sleep problems, a melodic alarm is a great alternative to standard beeping. Getting at least seven hours of sleep is still the best way to wake up feeling happy.
