I. Reading Passage
Fewer Products to Choose from Can Lead to Better Sales

Historically, businesses believed that offering a wide variety of products would attract more customers. However, recent studies show that having too many choices can overwhelm shoppers. When faced with 30 options of a single item, people feel stress [R] and spend a lot of time. This makes shopping very time-consuming. Sometimes, they leave the store without making a purchase.

To simplify the decision process, many modern businesses are starting to limit their options. For example, Coca-Cola decided to discontinue about half of its drinks. An automaker like General Motors also reduced the complexity of its car options to lower manufacturing costs.

Experts call this issue choice overload. When companies reduce the number of options, sales volume actually increases. For shoppers who practice budgeting [R], a smaller selection helps them make faster choices because they know exactly what they can afford [R]. This strategic change is a win-win for both stores and shoppers.

Source: Adapted from VOA Learning English - "Fewer Products to Choose from Can Lead to Better Sales" (February 14, 2024)
