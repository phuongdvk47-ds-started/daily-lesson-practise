I. Reading Passage
The Benefits of Volunteering for Older Adults

Volunteering in late life is a great way to help the community. A new study from UC Davis Health, which was presented at a conference in Amsterdam, shows that volunteering can also help older adults keep their brains healthy. Researchers studied 2,476 older people with an average age of 74. They found that older adults who volunteer have better cognitive function, especially executive function and verbal episodic memory. Executive function includes tasks like planning, focusing, and solving everyday problems in their leisure [R] time, without needing complex computer equipment [R]. Verbal episodic memory is the ability to remember lists of words or events.

The study is unique because the participants came from very diverse backgrounds. About 48% of the participants were Black, 20% were white, 17% were Asian, and 14% were Latino. Most earlier studies on brain health only looked at white populations. Rachel Whitmer, a professor at UC Davis, explained that volunteerism is important for cognitive aging in all communities. She encourages older adults of all backgrounds to consider volunteering. This activity can help their neighborhoods and also protect their own brain health.

The researchers also looked at whether volunteering could reduce [R] cognitive decline over 1.2 years. They found that people who volunteer several times per week had the highest levels of executive function. Volunteering might protect the brain because it offers cognitive stimulation, social interaction, and physical activity. Although more research is needed to prove if volunteering directly prevents dementia, the current findings show a positive relationship between community work and brain power.

Source: Adapted from UC Davis Health - "Volunteering in late life may protect the brain against cognitive decline and dementia" (2023-07-20)
URL: https://health.ucdavis.edu/news/headlines/volunteering-in-late-life-may-protect-the-brain-against-cognitive-decline-and-dementia/2023/07
