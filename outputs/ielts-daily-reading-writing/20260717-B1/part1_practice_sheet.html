I. Reading Passage
Why Reading Is Good for You

Many people enjoy reading for pleasure. They read novels, newspapers, articles, and websites every day. Reading is a popular hobby in almost every country in the world.

Reading is good exercise for the brain. When we read, our minds create mental simulations of the scenes in the story. This activity stimulates the brain and helps build new neural pathways. A study in Spain found that reading words related to smells, such as 'lavender', activated the brain's smell-processing areas.

Stories also help us understand other people. Reading fiction activates the brain networks that we use for social interaction. As a result, regular readers often develop more empathy and stronger emotional intelligence. They find it easier to see situations from another person's point of view.

Reading can also improve our health and happiness. Research shows that reading for just thirty minutes a week can increase a person's health and wellbeing. It can also improve confidence, help people sleep better, and reduce feelings of loneliness. A 2024 study by the Reading Agency found that people who read regularly reported higher life satisfaction and better concentration.

In addition, reading is one of the best ways to reduce stress. In a large study called the 'Rest Test', reading was ranked the number one stress reliever, beating nature, music, and being alone. Some research even suggests that reading stories may help people live longer. One study found that thirty minutes of daily reading was linked to a longer life, and this benefit was unique to narrative fiction.

Finally, reading gives the brain regular cognitive stimulation and may protect long-term brain health. Mental activity like reading might delay the onset of dementia in older adults. Reading also expands a person's vocabulary and general knowledge of the world. For all these reasons, picking up a book is a simple habit with lasting benefits.
