III. Writing Guidance / Suggested Answers
Task 1: Word Ordering (Sắp xếp từ thành câu hoàn chỉnh)
  • Our town library has many books.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Let's build this sentence together. In English, we start with the Subject (who or what has the books?). Our subject is "Our town library". Next, we need the Verb (what does it do?). The verb is "has" (singular because "library" is one library). Finally, we add the Object (what does it have?) which is "many books". Put them together, capitalize the first letter, and add a period: Our town library has many books.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Check if you capitalized "Our" and added a period . at the end.
Task 2: Sentence Completion (Hoàn thành câu)
  • 1. I like to go to the library because I can read many interesting books.
  • Teacher's Guidance: The prompt starts with "I like to go to the library because". "Because" must be followed by a full clause (Subject + Verb + Object) explaining your reason. For example, I (subject) + can read (verb phrase) + many interesting books (object). This gives: I like to go to the library because I can read many interesting books.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Check that your reason has both a subject ("I") and a verb ("can read").
  • 2. At the library, I can study my homework and use the computers.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Starting with "At the library, I can", the modal "can" must be followed by one or more base verbs. For example: study my homework and use the computers. This gives: At the library, I can study my homework and use the computers.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Ensure the verbs "study" and "use" are in their base form.
Task 3: Translation (Dịch câu)
  • 1. I often borrow books at the weekend. (or: I often borrow books on weekends.)
  • Teacher's Guidance: Let's translate: "Tôi" is "I" (subject). "thường" is the adverb of frequency "often" (placed before the main verb). "mượn sách" is "borrow books" (verb + object). "vào cuối tuần" is "at the weekend" or "on weekends". Put it together: I often borrow books at the weekend.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Make sure "often" is before the verb "borrow".
  • 2. The new library is very modern and quiet.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Let's translate: "Thư viện mới" is "The new library" (adjective "new" comes before the noun "library"). "rất hiện đại và yên tĩnh" is "is very modern and quiet" (using the linking verb "is" followed by the adjective phrase). Put it together: The new library is very modern and quiet.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Check the spelling of "quiet" (do not confuse it with "quite").
Task 4: Short Description (Mô tả ngắn)

Suggested Model Answer:

"The Central Library is in the city center. It has fifty thousand books. People can use free Wi-Fi and study rooms there." (22 words)
  • Teacher's Guidance: Let's learn how to write a short description based on facts! We can create three simple, clear sentences:

1. Sentence 1 (Name & Location): Combine the name and location. (The Central Library is in the city center.)

2. Sentence 2 (Capacity/Books): Describe the number of books. (It has fifty thousand books.)

3. Sentence 3 (Facilities/Activities): List what people can do. (People can use free Wi-Fi and study rooms there.)

By following this outline, you will write a very clean and professional description!

  • Self-Correction Tip: Verify that you wrote "is in the city center" (using preposition "in") and "It has" (not "It have").
Task 5: (*) Guided Email Invite (Viết email ngắn)

Suggested Model Answer:

"Hi Lan,
Would you like to study English with me at the Central Library this Sunday? It is a quiet place. We can meet there at 9:00 AM.
Write back soon!
Best,
Minh" (38 words)
  • Teacher's Guidance: Writing an invitation email is a vital real-life skill. Let's follow this standard 5-step template:

1. Greeting: Friendly opening (Hi Lan,).

2. The Invite: Ask your friend politely (Would you like to study English with me at the Central Library this Sunday?).

3. A Reason/Description: Describe the library (It is a quiet place.).

4. Meeting Time: Suggest a specific time (We can meet there at 9:00 AM.).

5. Sign-off: Close warmly (Best, followed by your name).

  • Self-Correction Tip: Remember to use "at" before time (at 9:00 AM) and place a comma after the greeting (Hi Lan,) and sign-off (Best,).
