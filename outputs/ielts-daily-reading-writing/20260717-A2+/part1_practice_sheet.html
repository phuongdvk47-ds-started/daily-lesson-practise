I. Reading Passage
How Music Lessons Help Young Minds

Attention and working memory are important parts of how we think. Attention helps us focus on one thing at a time, and working memory helps us hold information in our mind for a short time. Both of these thinking skills can be improved by training. Scientists have wanted to know which activities can make these skills stronger. One activity that has been shown to improve thinking skills is musical training. Learning to play an instrument is enjoyable, but it may also help the brain in other ways.

In a study, scientists compared two groups of children. Twenty of the children played a musical instrument, and they were between 10 and 13 years old. The other twenty children did not play any instrument. The scientists gave the children memory tasks and looked at how well they did. The children who had musical training performed better on the memory tasks than the children who did not play music.

Brain scans gave the scientists even more information. The scans showed that the young musicians had higher activity in the parts of the brain that control attention. There was also a clear pattern: the longer a child had trained, the stronger this brain activity was. This research suggests that learning music can bring a real cognitive benefit to children. Playing an instrument is not only fun; it may also help young people think and remember more clearly.

Source: Adapted from Frontiers in Neuroscience - "Neural Dynamics of Improved Bimodal Attention and Working Memory in Musically Trained Children" (2020-10-08)
URL: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnins.2020.554731/full
