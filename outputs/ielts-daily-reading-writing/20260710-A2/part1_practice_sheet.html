I. Reading Passage
Learning English for a Hotel Job

Many people want to work in the hotel industry because it is exciting and offers good careers. To get a hotel job, you need good English skills. However, you do not need perfect grammar. In fact, most hotel managers prefer staff who are polite, friendly, and ready to help guests. In addition, many hotels provide free on-the-job training, where new workers learn the standard rules and phrases. This training helps them become more independent [R] in their roles.

In a hotel, listening and speaking are the most important communication skills. Receptionists and other staff must talk to customers every day. Because guests come from many different countries, they often speak with different accents and at different speeds. Having a good ear helps workers understand reservations, check-ins, and the daily schedule [R] of the hotel. If staff listen carefully, they can avoid serious mistakes.

Fortunately, you can prepare for a hotel job in several simple ways. First, you can visit popular hotel websites and write down useful vocabulary. Second, watching educational videos about hotel English online is very helpful. Finally, you can use a digital planner [R] or app to manage your study time and practice listening to various accents by watching international travel programs. These steps will make you feel more confident when you apply for a job.
