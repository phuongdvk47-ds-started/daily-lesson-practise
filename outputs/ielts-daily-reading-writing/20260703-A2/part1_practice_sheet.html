I. Reading Passage
'Weekend Warriors' Exercise and Health Benefits

Many busy people find it difficult to exercise regularly during the week. Finding time to work out can be a big challenge [R] for adults with busy schedules. However, a study shows that people who compress all their exercise into one or two days—often called "weekend warriors"—still get major health benefits.

The study, published in JAMA Internal Medicine, analyzed data from more than 63,000 adults. Researchers compared active individuals with those who were physically inactive. They found that weekend warriors reduced their mortality risk by 30% compared to inactive people. Meanwhile, those who exercised frequently throughout the week lowered their risk by 35%. Both groups experienced a significant reduction in the risk of dying from cancer and cardiovascular disease.

The researchers conclude that even a limited amount of exercise, such as going for a strenuous run or doing active household chores on weekends, is enough to improve [R] health. This is encouraging news for those who want to participate [R] in sports but have very little time. It helps prevent obesity and build a healthy habit for life.

Source: Adapted from VOA News - "'Weekend Warriors' Shown to Lower Their Risk of Life-threatening Illnesses" (January 9, 2017)
