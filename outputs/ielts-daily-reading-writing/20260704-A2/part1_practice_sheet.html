I. Reading Passage
Learning English for a Hotel Job

Many adults [R] choose to work in the hotel industry. They do this because it is an exciting career with many opportunities to meet international visitors. To get a job at a hotel, you need to learn 'hotel English.' This is polite, spoken English that uses specific terms. Front-desk staff use this language to welcome guests, confirm a reservation, and handle a request for help. If you practice regularly [R], you will become more confident.

Some people think that they must speak perfect English to work in hospitality. However, hotel professional Mona Faal says this is not true. Perfect grammar is generally not mandatory for most jobs. Employers want candidates who are effective communicators and can provide outstanding service. Most hotels also provide on-the-job training for their staff. Therefore, having a good interaction with guests is more important than having no mistakes.

There are many ways to prepare for a hotel career. You can study hotel websites to learn about different rooms and amenities. You can also watch multimedia videos, such as online dialogues, to hear polite assistance phrases. Because guests come from all over the world, they will speak with different accents. Listening to travel programs can help you get used to different sounds. Through active language immersion, you can build a useful routine and gain valuable experience in speaking. We can conclude [R] that with the right preparation, anyone can succeed in this career.

Source: Adapted from VOA Learning English - "You Can Learn English for a Hotel Job" (June 29, 2019)
