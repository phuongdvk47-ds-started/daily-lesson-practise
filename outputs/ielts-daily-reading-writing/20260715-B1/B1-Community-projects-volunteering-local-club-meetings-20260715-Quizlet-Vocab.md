### Phần 1: Học từ vựng đơn giản (Simple Vocabulary)
| Từ vựng tiếng Anh | Nghĩa tiếng Việt |
| --- | --- |
| cognitive function | The mental process of acquiring knowledge and understanding through thought, experience, and the senses |
| executive function | Mental skills that include planning, focusing, remembering instructions, and juggling multiple tasks |
| episodic memory | The memory of every day events that can be explicitly stated or conjured |
| cognitive decline | A gradual decrease in mental abilities, such as memory and thinking speed |
| dementia | A chronic or persistent disorder of the mental processes caused by brain disease or injury |
| baseline score | A measurement that is taken at the beginning of an experiment or study for later comparison |
| diverse cohort | A group of people sharing a common characteristic who come from varied backgrounds |
| low-cost intervention | A cheap action or treatment taken to improve a medical or social situation |
| cognitive stimulation | Activities that challenge and exercise the brain, helping to keep it active |
| social interaction | The process of reciprocal influence between individuals in a community |
| physical activity | Bodily movement produced by skeletal muscles that requires energy expenditure |
| community participation | The engagement of individuals in activities that benefit their local society |
| socioeconomic factors | Social and economic characteristics that influence an individual's life outcomes |
| statistical significance | A mathematical measure showing that an experimental result is unlikely to have occurred by chance |
| natural defense | An organic or inherent protection against disease or harm |
| protective benefits | Advantages that guard against danger, damage, or decline |
| disparities | A great difference, especially one that is unfair or unequal |
| epidemiology | The branch of medicine which deals with the incidence, distribution, and control of diseases |
| volunteerism | The use or involvement of volunteer labor, especially in community services |
| brain health | The state of optimal cognitive, sensory, and motor function of the brain |
