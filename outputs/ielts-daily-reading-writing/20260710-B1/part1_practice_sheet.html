I. Reading Passage
The Role of Teamwork and Sportsmanship in Sports

Playing sports offers numerous physical benefits, including improved cardiovascular fitness and overall health. Regular physical activity can prevent chronic diseases like diabetes and heart problems. Beyond physical wellness, sports also get kids and teens active and away from screens, countering modern public health issues like obesity and social disengagement. Moreover, participation in sports is widely believed to promote self-esteem and teach valuable personal traits such as self-discipline, teamwork [R], and leadership. In a team environment, individuals must learn to cooperate and work toward a common goal. Additionally, sports serve as a platform for social interaction, allowing participants to build strong community relationships.

However, sports can either support or undermine social relations. While sports are widely praised for their educational and character-building value, the effects of socialization through sports can be highly controversial. For instance, intense competition sometimes feeds a socially destructive desire to win at all costs. Some observers view these outcomes with skepticism [R], arguing that the pressure to win can encourage young athletes to play unfairly, break rules, or cheat. Players may feel that their self-worth is tied only to victory, leading to high levels of anxiety. There is indeed some evidence that this propensity to cheat increases as players grow older and the level of competition rises.

To combat these negative effects, sports organizations must enforce [R] strict rules and actively promote good sportsmanship. Coaches, parents, and administrators play a vital role as socializing agents. If these adults emphasize effort, skill development, and fair play over final scores, they can have a persuasive [R] influence on young athletes. By doing so, they help them understand that respecting teammates, opponents, and referees is the true heart of any game. Ultimately, the lessons learned on the sports field are transferable to school, work, and personal relationships, helping individuals become cooperative and responsible members of society.
