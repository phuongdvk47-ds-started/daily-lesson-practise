# Barron Book Structure

## Expected Book Format — Barron's Essential Words for IELTS

### Overview
The book is organized into Units, each focused on a specific topic. Each unit contains:

1. **Unit Title & Topic** — Clear heading with topic name
2. **Reading Passage** — Academic text with labeled paragraphs (A, B, C, etc.)
3. **Questions** — Multiple types:
   - True/False/Not Given
   - Matching headings
   - Multiple choice
   - Sentence completion
   - Summary completion
4. **Vocabulary Focus** — Target words with definitions and examples
5. **Word Family** — Noun/verb/adjective/adverb forms of target words
6. **Answer Key** — Correct answers for all questions (may be in separate section at end of book)

### Page Layout Patterns
- Unit headings are typically bold, large font, centered or left-aligned
- Reading passages have paragraph labels (A, B, C...) at the start
- Questions are numbered sequentially within the unit
- Vocabulary tables may span multiple pages
- Answer keys may be grouped at the end of the book or per-unit

### Common Structural Patterns
- Unit number + topic: "Unit 1: Unique Animals"
- Reading passage title: "READING PASSAGE: [Title]" or "Reading: [Title]"
- Questions section: "QUESTIONS" or "Questions 1-13"
- Vocabulary: "VOCABULARY" or "Target Words"
- Word Family: "WORD FAMILIES" or "Word Forms"
- Answer Key: "ANSWER KEY" or "Answers"

### Challenges
- Some scanned PDFs have low OCR quality
- Answer keys may be separated from their unit
- Table formatting may not survive extraction
- IPA transcriptions may use special fonts
- Vietnamese translations (if present) require Unicode support
