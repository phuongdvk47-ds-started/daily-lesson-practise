I. Reading Passage
The Rise of Smart Home Technology

Smart home technology has transformed modern living by integrating interconnected devices that allow remote management of household systems. Through a home Wi-Fi network, appliances such as lighting, security cameras, and heating systems can communicate with a central hub or a smartphone app. This connectivity offers homeowners unprecedented convenience, enabling them to control their environment from anywhere in the world. Instead of manually adjusting individual devices, users can create automated routines that run seamlessly in the background.

One of the most significant advantages of this technology is energy efficiency. Smart thermostats and occupancy sensors learn a household's habits, ensuring that heating and cooling are only active when rooms are occupied. Some advanced systems even manage electricity loads by scheduling energy-intensive chores during off-peak hours. Research indicates that these smart habits can help households reduce their overall energy consumption by 15% to 30%. Family members can collaborate on a common goal [R] of reducing their environmental footprint by adopting these energy-saving habits.

Despite these benefits, setting up a smart home is not without challenges. Users frequently face interoperability issues, where devices from different manufacturers fail to communicate with each other. While adjusting to smart home apps requires some self-discipline [R] to avoid over-reliance on automation, the convenience is undeniable. However, the initial financial investment is often high, and the technical complexity of installing these systems creates a steep learning curve for non-technical users.

Furthermore, security and privacy concerns remain a major obstacle to widespread adoption. Because smart speakers and cameras continuously monitor household activities, they collect a vast amount of behavioral data. If manufacturers fail to encrypt this data or if users choose weak passwords, hackers can easily gain access. Ultimately, neglecting network security can undermine [R] the entire system, leaving personal data vulnerable to cyberthreats. Therefore, users must prioritize changing default passwords and updating device firmware regularly.
