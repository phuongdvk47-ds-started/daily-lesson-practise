I. Reading Passage
Digital Guides in Modern Museums

The British Museum Audio app is a digital guide designed to help visitors explore the Museum's vast collection. People can download this app directly onto their smartphones, allowing them to navigate the galleries easily or even explore the famous objects from home. The app includes expert commentaries written by the Museum's curatorial team. These commentaries explain the history and stories behind 250 highlights of the collection, offering visitors access to the latest research about these ancient artifacts.

To make a visit more enjoyable, the app provides introductions to 65 different galleries and features several self-guided themed tours. Visitors appreciate the convenience [R] of having a personal guide on their phone. Crucially, key commentaries are available in nine languages, including English, Chinese, French, and Japanese, as well as British Sign Language. This language variety helps international tourists understand the exhibits without needing a human translator, making the museum experience more inclusive for everyone.

Using the digital guide inside the galleries is straightforward. Visitors simply look for the audio guide symbol next to display cases or objects, then key in the object's number to hear the commentary. There are both advantages and disadvantages [R] to this technology. While it allows visitors to study exhibits at their own pace, they must bring their own headphones. Otherwise, they might disturb other people in the quiet gallery spaces.

Many visitors travel to the museum using public transport [R] because parking in London is extremely limited. Once inside, using the audio app can enrich their journey. However, visitors must ensure their phone batteries are fully charged before arrival. Ultimately, mobile applications like this one are changing how people interact with history, making cultural tours more accessible and educational for a new generation of learners who prefer digital learning over paper maps.
