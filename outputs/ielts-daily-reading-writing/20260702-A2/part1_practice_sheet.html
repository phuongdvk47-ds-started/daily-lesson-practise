I. Reading Passage
Debating Year-Round Education

This is an educational report about school calendars. Today, we discuss how schools prepare [R] students for the future. Some people say the traditional calendar of 180 days no longer meets the needs of modern society. They point out that students in most other industrial countries spend more hours a day in school.

Critics also say a long summer vacation causes students to forget much of what they learned. Schools are under pressure to raise test scores. Teachers recommend [R] changing calendars to improve student performance. Some schools have lengthened the school day or added more days to the year. This can be costly because school employees must be paid for the extra time, and families need to buy nutritious [R] school lunches. Local businesses may object to a longer year because students cannot work at summer jobs.

Some schools have a year-round schedule, where the school year is extended over twelve months. Instead of a long vacation, there are many short ones. Over five percent of public school students attend these year-round schools. In these schools, students learn many subjects and have short breaks to stay fresh.

Source: Adapted from VOA Learning English - "Debating Year-Round Education" (2007-08-29)
