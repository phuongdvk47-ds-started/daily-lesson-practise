I. Reading Passage
What Is the Healthiest Way to Prepare Vegetables?

How we prepare our food can change its nutritional value. Dietitians suggest that if you want to eat a nutritious meal, you should consider the method you use to cook your vegetables. They recommend steaming and microwaving as the healthiest ways because they preserve the most nutrients. Both methods are quick and require no added fat, making vegetables an affordable [R] option for everyone.

On the other hand, boiling vegetables for a long time can cause water-soluble nutrients—like vitamin C, vitamin B1, and folate—to leach into the cooking water. If you throw the water away, these vitamins are lost. However, you can repurpose the water for soups or drink it to get the benefit of the leached nutrients. Another good option is blanching, which involves boiling vegetables for only a minute or two.

Dietitians note that using a small amount of fat like olive oil is not a problem and can actually help your body absorb health-boosting antioxidants and vitamins. While cooking can reduce some nutrients, it also helps the body digest other vitamins, such as lycopene found in cooked tomatoes. Families who share [R] meals and recipes often find that preparing food at home is less expensive [R] and healthier than eating out.
