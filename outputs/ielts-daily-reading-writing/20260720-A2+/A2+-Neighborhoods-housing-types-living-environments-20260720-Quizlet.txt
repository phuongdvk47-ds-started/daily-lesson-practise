urban (/ˈɜː.bən/) [adjective]	Connected with a town or city - thuộc thành phố, đô thị. Ví dụ: More and more people are moving to urban areas for work.
suburban (/səˈbɜː.bən/) [adjective]	Connected with an area where people live that is outside the centre of a city - thuộc vùng ngoại ô. Ví dụ: Belfast has several quiet suburban towns nearby.
rural (/ˈrʊə.rəl/) [adjective]	Connected with the countryside rather than the town - thuộc nông thôn, thôn quê. Ví dụ: In rural areas, public transport is often limited.
suburbs (/ˈsʌb.ɜːbz/) [noun plural]	The outer parts of a town, where people live - khu vực ngoại ô. Ví dụ: Many families prefer to live in the suburbs because of more space.
settlement (/ˈset.əl.mənt/) [noun]	A place where people have come to live and make their homes - nơi định cư, khu định cư. Ví dụ: A hamlet is a very small settlement in the countryside.
hamlet (/ˈhæm.lət/) [noun]	A very small village that does not have its own church - thôn xóm, bản nhỏ. Ví dụ: They decided to buy a house in a quiet, isolated hamlet.
facilities (/fəˈsɪl.ə.tiz/) [noun plural]	Buildings, services, or equipment that are provided for a particular purpose - cơ sở vật chất, tiện ích. Ví dụ: The sports club has excellent modern facilities.
lifestyle (/ˈlaɪf.staɪl/) [noun]	The way in which a person or a group of people lives - lối sống, phong cách sống. Ví dụ: They want to change their lifestyle and live in a calmer environment.
transport (/ˈtræn.spɔːt/) [noun]	A system for carrying people or goods from one place to another - phương tiện giao thông, sự vận chuyển. Ví dụ: Buses and trains are forms of public transport.
advantage (/ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ/) [noun]	A feature that makes something better than other things - lợi thế, ưu điểm. Ví dụ: One advantage of rural life is cleaner air.
disadvantage (/ˌdɪs.ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ/) [noun]	A feature that makes something worse than other things - bất lợi, nhược điểm. Ví dụ: A major disadvantage of the village is the lack of shops.
crowded (/ˈkraʊ.dɪd/) [adjective]	Having a lot of people or too many people - đông đúc. Ví dụ: City buses are often crowded during rush hours.
distraction (/dɪˈstræk.ʃən/) [noun]	Something that takes your attention away from what you are doing - sự xao nhãng, làm mất tập trung. Ví dụ: It is hard to work at home because there are too many distractions.
commute (/kəˈmjuːt/) [verb]	To travel regularly between your home and your place of work - đi lại đều đặn hàng ngày. Ví dụ: Many people commute from the suburbs to Belfast for work.
working memory (/ˈwɜː.kɪŋ ˈmem.ər.i/) [noun phrase]	Memory used for a short time - trí nhớ làm việc. Ví dụ: Working memory helps us hold information in our mind for a short time.
musical training (/ˈmjuː.zɪ.kəl ˈtreɪ.nɪŋ/) [noun phrase]	Learning to play music - sự luyện tập âm nhạc. Ví dụ: Musical training can improve a child's thinking skills.
cognitive (/ˈkɒɡ.nə.tɪv/) [adjective]	Connected with thinking and learning - thuộc về nhận thức. Ví dụ: Learning music can bring a real cognitive benefit to children.
have access to (/hæv ˈæk.ses tuː/) [phrase]	To have the opportunity to use or see something - tiếp cận, sử dụng được cái gì. Ví dụ: Residents in cities have easy access to healthcare.
in contrast (/ɪn kənˈtrɑːst/) [phrase]	Used to show a clear difference between two things - ngược lại, trái lại. Ví dụ: Suburbs are quiet. In contrast, downtown areas are very noisy.
depend on (/dɪˈpend ɒn/) [phrase]	To need or rely on somebody/something - phụ thuộc vào. Ví dụ: Public transport in rural villages depends on a single bus line.
