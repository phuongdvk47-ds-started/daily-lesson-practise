I. Reading Passage
Small Living Space Returns as Housing Prices Rise in US

As American cities face rising costs, small living spaces are returning to the housing market. These very small units, called micro-apartments, are becoming popular in cities like Seattle, Portland, and New York. Historically, these were known as single-room occupancy units, or SROs.

After World War II, many of these small rooms disappeared. Developers built larger homes, and people moved to the suburbs. Between the early 1900s and 2014, New York City lost about 70,000 single-room units. However, because of a housing shortage and expensive rents today, cities are investing in small spaces again.

A micro-apartment is a small, private space. In Seattle, some units measure only 17 square meters. While they do not have much room, they are affordable. For example, one unit rent is $900 per month, which is much lower than a standard studio apartment.

Experts say this type of housing is a good alternative to homelessness or overcrowded living conditions. It helps people who cannot afford normal apartments. A family in Seattle said that despite the small space, they love their home because of its proximity to family, food stores, and other neighborhood amenities. They also share a kitchen and laundry rooms with other residents to save money.

Source: Adapted from VOA Learning English - "Small Living Space Returns as Housing Prices Rise in US" (March 25, 2024)
