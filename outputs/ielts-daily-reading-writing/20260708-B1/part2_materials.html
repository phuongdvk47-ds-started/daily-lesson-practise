II. Grammar Detail & Strategy (Lý thuyết & Mẹo)
Chủ điểm 1: Card 1: Passive Voice Basics

The Passive Voice focuses on the action or the object receiving the action. Form: Subject + BE + Past Participle (V3). Example: 'Young consumers are targeted by ads' (not: 'Young consumers target by ads').

Chủ điểm 2: Card 2: Contrast Linkers

Use while or whereas to connect two contrasting clauses in one sentence (e.g. 'Adults ignore ads, whereas children watch them'). Use By contrast, or In contrast, at the beginning of a new sentence.

III. Common Mistakes / IELTS Traps for This Topic
Common mistake / Trap Wrong example Correct version Why it matters for IELTS
Forgetting the 'be' verb in passive sentences The laws passed last year. The laws were passed last year. Without 'be', the sentence sounds like the laws did the action themselves.
Using 'whereas' as a separate sentence Children like toys. Whereas adults like cars. Children like toys, whereas adults like cars. 'Whereas' is a conjunction; it must connect two clauses within the same sentence.
