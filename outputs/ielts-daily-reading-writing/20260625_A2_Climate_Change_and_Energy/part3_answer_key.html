III. Writing Guidance / Suggested Answers
Task 1: Rearrange the words to make correct sentences (Questions 1-2)
  • 1. Wind power is a clean energy source.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Let's look at how we build this sentence together. In English, we always start with the Subject (who or what does the action?). Here, our subject is "Wind power". Next, we need the Verb (what does it do or what is it?). The verb is "is". Finally, we add the complement description "a clean energy source" (the adjective phrase "clean energy" comes before the noun "source"). Put them together: Wind power is a clean energy source.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Check if you capitalized "Wind" and placed a period . at the end.
  • 2. We must reduce burning fossil fuels.
  • Teacher's Guidance: Let's identify the subject: "We". Next, we use the modal verb "must" followed by a base verb "reduce". After "reduce", we use a gerund (V-ing): "burning fossil fuels". Combining these gives us: We must reduce burning fossil fuels.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Ensure you wrote "must reduce" without adding "to", and used "burning" (gerund) after the verb "reduce".
Task 2: Complete the sentence using your own words (Question 3)
  • 3. We can use solar panels to generate electricity.
  • Teacher's Guidance: The prompt starts with "We can use". After "use", we need an object: "solar panels" or "solar power". To show the purpose, we use "to + base verb": "to generate electricity". This gives us a beautiful, rich sentence: We can use solar panels to generate electricity.
  • Self-Correction Tip: Verify that "generate" is in its base form after "to", and "electricity" is spelled correctly.
