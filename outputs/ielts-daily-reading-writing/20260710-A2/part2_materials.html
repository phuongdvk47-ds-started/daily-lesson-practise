II. Grammar Detail & Strategy (Lý thuyết & Mẹo)
Chủ điểm 1: Past Simple

We use the past simple to describe completed actions in the past. Regular verbs end in -ed (e.g. booked, stayed). Irregular verbs change form (e.g. spoke, had, was/were).

Tài liệu tự học trực tuyến uy tín:

  • British Council - Present Perfect vs Past Simple
  • Perfect English Grammar - Present Perfect or Past Simple
Chủ điểm 2: Modals of Advice and Obligation
  • must / have to: Strong obligation or rules (e.g. You must show passport).
  • should: Advice or recommendations (e.g. You should book early).
  • can / cannot: Ability or permission (e.g. You can call front desk, You cannot smoke).
III. Common Mistakes / IELTS Traps for This Topic
Common mistake / Trap Wrong example Correct version Why it matters for IELTS
Adding 'to' after 'must' or 'should' Guests must to pay now. Guests must pay now. Modals must be followed directly by a bare infinitive.
Using present tense in past contexts Yesterday, we check in late. Yesterday, we checked in late. Past actions require past simple verbs.
