# Grammar Targets by CEFR Level

Choose 1-3 targets per daily pack. Recycle a recent weak target when learner history is available.

## A1
- be, have, there is/there are
- present simple for routines and facts
- basic subject-verb agreement
- singular/plural nouns and articles a/an/the
- possessive adjectives and possessive 's
- basic prepositions of place/time
- can/can't for ability
- simple conjunctions: and, but, because

## A2
- present simple vs present continuous
- past simple regular/irregular verbs
- future with going to and will for simple plans
- comparatives and superlatives
- basic data comparison modifiers for early Task 1: significantly, slightly, much, a little, twice as, half as, higher/lower than
- countable/uncountable nouns, some/any/much/many
- modals: should, must, have to, can/could
- adverbs of frequency and manner
- compound sentences with because/so/although

## B1
- present perfect vs past simple
- past continuous and narrative sequencing
- first and second conditionals
- passive voice basic forms
- relative clauses: who/which/that/where
- defining vs non-defining relative clauses, including comma use and meaning difference
- gerunds and infinitives
- reported speech basics
- linking devices for cause, contrast, result
- contrast linkers for simple data/chart writing: while, whereas, by contrast, in contrast

## B2
- complex sentences with concession, purpose, result
- advanced passive structures
- noun phrases and nominalisation basics
- reduced relative clauses
- participle clauses introduction
- mixed conditionals introduction
- hedging with may/might/tend to/appear to
- referencing and substitution: this/these/such/one/ones
- advanced subject-verb agreement with complex subjects and intervening phrases
- articles with geographical names, institutions, abstract concepts, social/economic groups, and academic generalisations
- parallel structures and sentence combining

## C1
- advanced nominalisation
- inversion for emphasis
- cleft sentences
- participle clauses for concision
- complex conditionals and alternatives to if
- subjunctive mood for recommendations and formal arguments: it is essential that governments implement, it is vital that schools provide
- hedging and stance: arguably, seemingly, to some extent
- advanced cohesive referencing
- grammar for counterargument and concession

## C2
- stylistic control and register shifting
- dense noun phrases
- advanced subordination and embedding
- rhetorical emphasis and fronting
- nuanced modality and epistemic stance
- subjunctive mood and formal mandative structures for policy recommendations, academic proposals, and high-register argumentation
- parallelism and contrastive structures
- precision editing for ambiguity and concision
- complex punctuation for academic prose


## High-impact IELTS Error Priorities
Use these targets proactively because they often reduce Writing accuracy even when the learner has good ideas:
- A2: data comparison modifiers for early chart language, especially `slightly/significantly` and ratio expressions such as `twice as...as`.
- B1: defining vs non-defining relative clauses; comma placement changes meaning and formality.
- B1: contrast linkers for simple chart comparison: `while`, `whereas`, `by contrast`, `in contrast`.
- B2: subject-verb agreement when the head noun is separated from the verb by phrases such as `along with`, `as well as`, `of...`, or relative clauses.
- B2: articles with places, institutions, abstract concepts, and academic plural generalisations; include error-correction items for `a/an/the/zero article`.
- C1-C2: subjunctive mood after recommendation/necessity adjectives and verbs, especially in formal Task 2 policy arguments.
