advertiser (/ˈæd.və.taɪ.zər/) [noun]	A person or company that advertises a product or service - nhà quảng cáo. Ví dụ: The advertiser spent a lot of money on the new campaign.
persuasive (/pəˈsweɪ.sɪv/) [adjective]	Good at making someone do or believe something through reasoning - có sức thuyết phục. Ví dụ: The commercial was very persuasive.
targeted (/ˈtɑː.ɡɪ.tɪd/) [adjective]	Directed at a particular group or activity - được nhắm mục tiêu. Ví dụ: The company uses targeted marketing to reach teens.
celebrity endorsement (/səˈleb.rə.ti ɪnˈdɔːs.mənt/) [noun phrase]	The use of a famous person to advertise a product - sự quảng cáo có người nổi tiếng. Ví dụ: Celebrity endorsement is a powerful tool in advertising.
inadequacy (/ɪˈnæd.ɪ.kwə.si/) [noun]	The state of not being good enough or confident enough - sự thiếu thốn, sự kém cỏi. Ví dụ: Ads can cause feelings of inadequacy in young people.
obesity (/əʊˈbiː.sə.ti/) [noun]	The condition of being extremely fat or overweight in a way that is dangerous for health - bệnh béo phì. Ví dụ: Childhood obesity is linked to junk food commercials.
media literacy (/ˈmiː.di.ə ˈlɪt.ər.ə.si/) [noun phrase]	The ability to critically analyze and evaluate media messages - sự hiểu biết về truyền thông. Ví dụ: Schools should teach media literacy to students.
manipulate (/məˈnɪp.jə.leɪt/) [verb]	To control something or someone to your advantage, often unfairly - thao túng. Ví dụ: Companies try to manipulate consumers' choices.
regulate (/ˈreɡ.jə.leɪt/) [verb]	To control something, especially by making it work in a particular way - quy định, quản lý. Ví dụ: Governments need to regulate online ads.
prohibited (/prəˈhɪb.ɪ.tɪd/) [adjective]	Forbidden by law, rule, or other authority - bị cấm. Ví dụ: Advertising junk food to children is prohibited here.
commercial motive (/kəˈmɜː.ʃəl ˈməʊ.tɪv/) [noun phrase]	A reason for doing something that is related to making a profit - động cơ thương mại. Ví dụ: Children often do not understand the commercial motive behind games.
audience (/ˈɔː.di.əns/) [noun]	The group of people together in one place to watch or listen to a play, film, someone speaking, etc. - khán giả, người xem. Ví dụ: The ad targets a young audience.
stealth marketing (/stelθ ˈmɑː.kɪ.tɪŋ/) [noun phrase]	The practice of promoting products to people without them knowing they are being marketed to - tiếp thị tàng hình, quảng cáo giấu mặt. Ví dụ: Influencers often use stealth marketing in their videos.
free trade (/friː treɪd/) [noun phrase]	International buying and selling of goods, without limits on the amount of goods that one country can sell to another - thương mại tự do. Ví dụ: Some argue that bans violate the principles of free trade.
enforce (/ɪnˈfɔːs/) [verb]	To make people obey a law, or to make a particular situation happen or be accepted - thực thi, ép buộc. Ví dụ: The government must enforce strict advertising rules.
purchasing decision (/ˈpɜː.tʃəs.ɪŋ dɪˈsɪʒ.ən/) [noun phrase]	The thought process that leads a consumer from identifying a need to acting on it - quyết định mua hàng. Ví dụ: Ads have a big impact on a teenager's purchasing decision.
billboard (/ˈbɪl.bɔːd/) [noun]	A very large board on which advertisements are shown - biển quảng cáo. Ví dụ: There is a huge billboard on the highway.
customized (/ˈkʌs.tə.maɪzd/) [adjective]	Made or changed according to the buyer's or user's needs - được tùy chỉnh. Ví dụ: Online ads are highly customized for each user.
cognitive maturity (/ˈkɒɡ.nə.tɪv məˈtʃʊə.rə.ti/) [noun phrase]	The ability to think, learn, and remember at an adult level - sự trưởng thành về nhận thức. Ví dụ: Young kids lack the cognitive maturity to spot fake ads.
skepticism (/ˈskep.tɪ.sɪ.zəm/) [noun]	An attitude of doubting that claims or statements are true - sự hoài nghi. Ví dụ: Consumers should view online ads with a healthy skepticism.
