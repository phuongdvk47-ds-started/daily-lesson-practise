collection (/kəˈlekʃn/) [noun]	A group of objects that have been collected together - bộ sưu tập. Ví dụ: The British Museum houses a vast collection of ancient artifacts.
curator (/kjʊəˈreɪtə/) [noun]	A person whose job is to look after the objects in a museum - giám tuyển, người quản lý bảo tàng. Ví dụ: The museum curator explained the history of the Egyptian statues.
commentary (/ˈkɒməntri/) [noun]	A spoken description of what is happening or the stories behind objects - lời thuyết minh, lời bình luận. Ví dụ: The audio app provides an interesting commentary on each exhibit.
artifact (/ˈɑːtɪfækt/) [noun]	An object that is made by a person, especially one of historical interest - cổ vật, hiện vật lịch sử. Ví dụ: Ancient clay pots are valuable artifacts for understanding early humans.
exhibit (/ɪɡˈzɪbɪt/) [noun]	An object or a collection of objects put in a public place for people to see - vật trưng bày, triển lãm. Ví dụ: Please do not touch the exhibits inside the museum.
gallery (/ˈɡæləri/) [noun]	A room or building for showing works of art or historical objects - phòng trưng bày. Ví dụ: The museum has a special gallery for Greek sculptures.
themed tour (/θiːmd tʊə/) [noun]	A guided route focused on a specific topic or historical period - tour tự dẫn theo chủ đề. Ví dụ: We followed a themed tour of Roman history using the application.
navigation (/ˌnævɪˈɡeɪʃn/) [noun]	The act of directing or finding the way through a place - sự điều hướng, định vị đường đi. Ví dụ: The map on the app makes gallery navigation very simple.
display case (/dɪˈspleɪ keɪs/) [noun]	A glass box used to show and protect valuable objects in a museum - tủ kính trưng bày. Ví dụ: Gold jewelry was placed inside a secure display case.
signpost (/ˈsaɪnpəʊst/) [noun]	A sign that gives information or directions to visitors - biển chỉ dẫn. Ví dụ: Look at the signpost to find the way to the main exit.
highlight (/ˈhaɪlaɪt/) [noun]	The most interesting, important, or memorable part of something - điểm nổi bật, hiện vật tiêu biểu. Ví dụ: The Rosetta Stone is a major highlight of the museum collection.
interactive (/ˌɪntərˈæktɪv/) [adjective]	Allowing information to be passed between a user and a machine - tương tác. Ví dụ: The children loved playing with the interactive screens in the science museum.
monument (/ˈmɒnjʊmənt/) [noun]	A building or statue built to remind people of a famous person or event - di tích, đài kỷ niệm. Ví dụ: Ancient stone temples are national monuments that attract tourists.
guided tour (/ˈɡaɪdɪd tʊə/) [noun]	A visit to a place led by an expert who explains what you see - tour có hướng dẫn viên. Ví dụ: We booked a guided tour of the castle to learn its secrets.
audio guide (/ˈɔːdiəʊ ɡaɪd/) [noun]	A recorded commentary that visitors listen to while walking through a museum - thiết bị thuyết minh tự động. Ví dụ: The museum offers a free audio guide in several languages.
heritage (/ˈherɪtɪdʒ/) [noun]	History, traditions, and buildings that are part of a country's culture - di sản văn hóa. Ví dụ: Protecting historical monuments helps preserve our cultural heritage.
visitor flow (/ˈvɪzɪtə fləʊ/) [noun]	The movement of visitors through a museum layout to avoid crowding - luồng di chuyển của khách. Ví dụ: The new room layout was designed to improve visitor flow.
convenience (/kənˈviːniəns/) [noun]	The state of being useful, easy, or helpful for someone - sự tiện lợi. Ví dụ: Digital applications offer great convenience for travelers.
public transport (/ˈpʌblɪk ˈtrænspɔːt/) [noun]	Buses, trains, and subways that are available to the public - phương tiện giao thông công cộng. Ví dụ: Using public transport is cheaper than renting a car.
advantages and disadvantages (/ədˈvɑːntɪdʒɪz ænd ˌdɪsədˈvɪəntɪdʒɪz/) [phrase]	Pros and cons; beneficial and harmful factors of something - thuận lợi và khó khăn. Ví dụ: Living in a large city has both advantages and disadvantages.
